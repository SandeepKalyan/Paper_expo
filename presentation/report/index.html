01 · The problem

Vessel segmentation, but equivariant.

Vessels have no canonical orientation or scale. A standard 2-D convolution learns one oriented filter and relies on data augmentation to approximate rotational symmetry. RSF-Conv builds that symmetry into the operator itself by parameterising each filter as a Fourier series over rotations and a radial scaling. Dropping it into a U-Net gives a model with 13.9 % of the parameters that matches or beats the plain backbone on three retinal benchmarks.

Submission goal, three parts:

  • Port the operator faithfully into src/models/rsf_paper/.
  • Reproduce DRIVE numbers, then beat them via ensemble + TTA + threshold sweep.
  • Transfer: train on DRIVE, test on STARE and CHASE_DB1 — zero fine-tuning.

02 · Architecture

U-Net with every conv replaced by RSF-Conv.

4 encoder levels, bottleneck, 4 decoder levels, skip connections — nothing exotic at the macro scale. The contribution sits at the operator level: every Conv2d becomes an RSF-Conv with rotNum=8, numS=4, which synthesises 32 oriented-and-scaled filter variants per learnable kernel at forward time, then group-pools over rotations for equivariant features.

03 · Plan & infra

Parallel two-box pipeline.

One vast.ai A100 and one local laptop, running in parallel. Two seeds train on the A100 for an ensemble; the laptop holds an early-stopped fallback checkpoint. A shell queue runs eval → ensemble → OOD unattended so I come back to finished numbers instead of babysitting.
